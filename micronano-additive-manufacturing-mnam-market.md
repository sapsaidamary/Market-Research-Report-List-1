<p><p>Micro/Nano Additive Manufacturing (MNAM) refers to advanced fabrication techniques that create structures at the micro and nano scale using additive processes. The market can be categorized into Two-Photon Polymerization (TPP) and Non-Two-Photon Polymerization (Non-TPP). TPP employs focused laser beams to polymerize materials with high precision, allowing for intricate designs, while Non-TPP encompasses methods like inkjet or extrusion printing, which are adept at producing larger microstructures but with less fine detail compared to TPP.</p></p>
