<p><p>Fiber Grade Polylactic Acid (PLA) is a biodegradable polymer used to create fibers for various applications, including textiles and packaging. The market is primarily divided into two types: monofilament and multifilament. Monofilament consists of a single, continuous fiber, ideal for applications requiring strength and rigidity, such as fishing lines and textiles. Multifilament, on the other hand, comprises multiple intertwined fibers, offering flexibility and softness, making it suitable for applications like garments and non-woven fabrics. Both types leverage PLA's eco-friendly properties.</p></p>
