<p><p>The RTU porcelain enamel market is primarily segmented into two types: enamel powder and enamel frit. Enamel powder is a finely-ground material that is mixed with water to create a slurry for application on metal surfaces. Enamel frit, on the other hand, refers to glass particles fused at high temperatures, providing a smooth, durable finish. Both types are essential for creating protective and aesthetic coatings in various applications, including appliances, architectural elements, and cookware.</p></p>
