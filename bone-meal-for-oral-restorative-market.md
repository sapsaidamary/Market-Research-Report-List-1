<p><p>Bone meal for oral restorative applications comes in two primary types: autogenous tooth powder and xenogeneic beef bone meal. Autogenous tooth powder, derived from a patient's own dental tissue, promotes better integration and minimizes rejection risks. Conversely, xenogeneic beef bone meal originates from animal sources, providing a readily available substitute for bone grafting procedures. Both types aim to enhance bone regeneration in dental restorations, but their origin and processing significantly influence their biocompatibility and clinical outcomes.</p></p>
