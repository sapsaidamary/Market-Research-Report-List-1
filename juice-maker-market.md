<p><ul><li>Centrifugal</li><li>Masticating</li><li>Triturating</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The juice maker market features three primary types: centrifugal, masticating, and triturating juicers. Centrifugal juicers use fast-spinning blades to extract juice, delivering quick results but often losing nutrients. Masticating juicers operate at slower speeds, crushing fruits and vegetables to retain more nutrients and yield higher juice volumes. Triturating juicers, also known as twin-gear juicers, use two gears to slowly grind produce, resulting in the highest nutrient retention and juice quality, but they are typically more expensive and complex.</p></p>
