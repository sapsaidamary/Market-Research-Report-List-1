<p><p>The Low Voltage Disconnect Switch market is segmented into two primary types: fused and non-fused switches. Fused disconnect switches incorporate a fuse to provide overcurrent protection, ensuring safety during electrical faults. They are ideal for applications requiring additional safety measures. Non-fused switches, on the other hand, do not have fuses and are used primarily for disconnecting circuits where overcurrent protection is managed separately. Both types serve essential roles in ensuring electrical system reliability and safety across various industries.</p></p>
