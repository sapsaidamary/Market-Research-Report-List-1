<p><p>High-temperature furnaces are categorized into three main types: box furnaces, tube furnaces, and vacuum furnaces. Box furnaces feature a chamber design accommodating various sizes of materials for diverse heating applications. Tube furnaces, characterized by their cylindrical tubes, provide uniform heating for samples and are ideal for processing small quantities. Vacuum furnaces operate in a controlled environment, minimizing oxidation and contamination, making them suitable for high-purity applications. Together, these furnace types serve various industries, including materials science, metallurgy, and ceramics.</p></p>
