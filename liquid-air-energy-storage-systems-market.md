<p><p>Liquid Air Energy Storage Systems (LAESS) utilize the process of liquefying air to store energy. When excess energy is available, air is cooled to cryogenic temperatures to become liquid, which stores energy efficiently. When energy is needed, the liquid air is warmed, expanding back into gas and driving a turbine to generate electricity. This technology is gaining traction due to its potential for large-scale energy storage and its environmentally friendly characteristics.</p><p>The Liquid Air Energy Storage Systems Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 13.7% during the forecast period.
