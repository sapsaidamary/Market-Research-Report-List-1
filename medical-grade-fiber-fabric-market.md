<p><p>The medical grade fiber fabric market is categorized into three main types: non-woven, woven, and knitted fabrics. Non-woven fabrics are made by bonding fibers through mechanical, chemical, or thermal means, offering high breathability and fluid resistance, ideal for surgical gowns and drapes. Woven fabrics are constructed by interlacing yarns, providing durability and strength, commonly used in surgical textiles. Knitted fabrics, made by interlocking loops of yarn, offer flexibility and comfort, making them suitable for various medical applications like bandages and support garments.</p></p>
