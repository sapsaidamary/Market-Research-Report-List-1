<p><ul><li>Sensor Composed of Pressure-sensitive Material</li><li>Traditional Conductor Flexible Sensor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The tactile imaging market features two primary sensor types: pressure-sensitive material sensors and traditional conductor flexible sensors. Pressure-sensitive material sensors utilize specialized materials that respond to varying levels of pressure, enabling the detection of tactile information through deformation. Traditional conductor flexible sensors, on the other hand, employ conductive materials arranged in flexible formats, allowing them to detect changes in resistance due to applied pressure. These sensors are critical for applications in robotics, prosthetics, and healthcare, enhancing human-machine interaction through tactile feedback.</p></p>
